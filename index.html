WHO ARE WE

Gentle Souls Foundation is a non-governmental organization located at Atwima Boko in the Ashanti Region of Ghana focused at developing women and adolescent girls. Registered with the Registrar General’s Department of Ghana in December 2020, we aim to empower women and girls in the area of education and to inculcate in them the spirit of entrepreneurship and self-help initiatives to help improve their living conditions and reduce poverty. Due the economic hardships and general low living conditions women and especially girls usually face in the Atwima Boko environment, most of them resort to undignified lifestyles (especially sexual relationship with men) that ends up worsening their conditions, if not ruining their entire lives. In the Atwima Boko environment, several girls live in deplorable conditions that affect their education and general wellbeing. Low-income families struggle to provide adequately for the basic needs of girls, heightening period poverty, school dropouts and unwanted pregnancies and their rippling effects. It is in view of this that GSF was established to provide material, emotional and educational support systems to women and girls in low living conditions to improve their situation. Also, at GSF, we discuss pertinent issues concerning women such a menstruation and its associated problems, breast cancer and general health and wellbeing for women. Other areas of our work are single parenting, teenage pregnancies, livelihood for women, among others. Currently, we have 70 registered members from whom 20 are orphans living with their grandparents. Another 30 of them are products of teenage pregnancies who they are living with their single parents. Again, 10 are needy but brilliant students whom GSF has put under a scholarship scheme through Child Advocacy Foundation. For the remaining 10, 5 of them are from families with financial difficulties and 5 are teenage mothers who are under apprenticeship in dressmaking, soapmaking, beadmaking, etc. under the funding of GSF.

Our Mission

To inspire positive change through teaching and coaching whiles encouraging, engaging and equipping women and girls to believe in the possibilities.

Our Vision

To provide safe, supportive place where women and girls can overcome barriers, build positive relationships and develop confidence and skills for life.

Our Objectives

  1. Support the education of girls, especially those affect by life’s unfortunate circumstances.
  2. Empower needy and under-privileged adolescent girls to be less dependent.
  3. Create livelihood opportunities for women and girls

SOME ACTIVITIES ORGANIZED

Breast Cancer Awareness

Under women’s health and wellbeing is our annual breast cancer screening which began in 2021. In 2023, in collaboration with Peace and Love Hospital, GSF held the breast screening for about 500 women and girls at Atwima Yabe in the Atwima Kwanwoma District. At the end of event, 3 of those screened turned out positive and were referred to the hospital for treatment.

Menstrual Hygiene Awareness

Also, we regularly organize educational sessions for girls on menstrual hygiene days. In 2023, in collaboration with Akoma Hospital, we observed the menstrual hygiene day in the Atwima Boko community where we distributed 100 pieces of sanitary pad to girls affected by period poverty after extensive sensitization on menstrual hygiene.

Beneficiaries

Some beneficiaries of our programmes

Rebecca Acheampong

Rebecca came with her sister during the menstrual hygiene day sensitization and sanitary pad distribution in 2023. Unfortunately, they couldn’t get to the venue on time and so she did not get some of the sanitary pads we distributed. She began crying. Upon interrogation, she narrated her story that her father is down with stroke and has been on sick bed for about 3 years now. Family resources have been spent on her father's sickness but to no avail. As a result, all her younger siblings had to quit school. However, she was determined to continue schooling. She then resorted to selling porridge for a food vendor who gave her GHC 10 cedis daily. GSF followed up to her house and realized at first hand the deplorable condition in which her family lived. Through GSF partnership with Girls Advocacy Coalition, Rebecca received a scholarship and is currently studying at the Senior High School. .

Doris Appiah

Doris is a very talented poet and a spoken word artist. After both parents died, she was left to fend for herself which led to her dropping out of school. After knowing her story, GSF has reenrolled her in school and is currently in year 3 in junior high school. She is also a beneficiary of our skills training programme.

Esther Agyemang

Esther going to school when her father had an accident and couldn’t continue his work as a driver for the past 2 years. Coming from a family of 5, Esther had to sell corn dough for a petty trader, earning GHC 10 daily to take care of herself and her ailing father. Due to the economic situation, she had to quit school and focus on the petty trading, yet earning meagre amounts inadequate to take care of her family. Currently, GSF has enrolled her in school and has also taught her vocational skills for income-generation through our regular skills training sessions.
